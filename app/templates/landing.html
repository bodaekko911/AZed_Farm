The Farm Challenge

Farm operations move fast. Manual tracking makes them harder to control.

A farm business does not only sell products. It receives harvest, transforms raw materials, moves stock, supplies B2B clients, sells retail, records expenses, and needs reports. When every step lives in a different file or chat, managers lose visibility.

01

You receive harvest but lose the full picture later

Farm intake, sorting, losses, production use, and final stock are often tracked separately, making it hard to know what really happened to each kilo.

02

Production changes stock but the team updates it manually

Processing olives, herbs, powders, ferments, or packaged goods creates new stock and consumes raw materials. Without a system, inventory becomes inaccurate quickly.

03

Retail, B2B, and expenses do not speak to each other

POS sales, wholesale invoices, client payments, operational costs, and stock reports become different versions of the truth.

04

Sustainability data is hard to prove

Carbon, transport, inputs, and operational impact are difficult to track when data is spread across receipts, spreadsheets, and memory.
